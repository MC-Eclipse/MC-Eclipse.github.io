Valley North Origins

  • Founded in 1985 by visionary horticulturist Jane Doe, Valley North Nursery began as a ten-acre plot in Arizona's small town Cave Creek. Jane, who had spent years studying plant science and sustainable growing methods, dreamed of creating a place where high-quality, regionally adapted plants could flourish. With just a small propagation greenhouse, a handful of employees, and an unwavering passion for botany, she launched the nursery with an initial collection of cactus, succulents, and shrubs.
  • As the nursery grew through the 1990s, it became a warm and welcoming place for local families and garden lovers. Jane filled the grounds with new varieties of plants, colorful art, and friendly staff who were always happy to help newcomers pick the right plants. Weeknight classes such as Scouts learning and hands-on planting days became community favorites, drawing visitors who enjoyed learning simple gardening tips and spending time together outdoors.
  • Over the decades, Valley North Nursery has blossomed into a beloved community institution, expanding its offerings to include a diverse array of plants suited for desert and drought-tolerant landscaping. Today, the nursery features an extensive selection of native plants, ornamental trees, and garden accessories. Despite its growth, Valley North remains committed to Jane's original vision of promoting community gardening practices and providing exceptional customer service to gardeners of all experience levels.
